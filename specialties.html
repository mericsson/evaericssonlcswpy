Individual Therapy

One doesn’t need to have serious psychological problems to seek counseling.

In fact, most people come to therapy depressed, anxious, upset or “stuck” because life has become a little too much. Too much stress. Too much change. Too many (ungrateful) people to please. Not enough time or (perhaps meaning) to make it all worthwhile.

This level of stress takes its toll on all our relationships, including our relationship with our self.

That’s when we need to take a step back, breathe, and talk to somebody to help gain a new perspective on the situation. When we can safely evaluate our choices so far, we can decide to choose differently in the future, creating a tomorrow that is more satisfying and compatible with who we truly are.

Therapy is about taking that break and getting new insight into what’s possible for your life.

Adolescent Therapy

The landscape of American adolescence is more competitive than ever, riddled with potential troubles from academic underachievement, drug (or video game) addiction, depression, and non-compliance with all things parental.

Still, most young people very much wish to achieve, contribute and belong.

Adolescent therapy can allow teens to reveal their fears and frustrations in a safe environment, and to learn new ways of seeing—and coping with--the pressures of growing up today.

Couples Therapy

This most intimate relationship has the greatest potential for joy, for growth, and for pain.

Using all available therapeutic methods, Eva helps bring peace to the current relationship to create an environment in which hurts can be forgiven, truths can be shared, and plans can be made anew.

Eva’s Gottman Institute training helps bring practical insight in the form of 30 years of marital research suggesting that there are solutions to marital problems that are proven to work. Learning these techniques greatly enhances the quality of any couple’s relationship by enabling greater respect, communication, and compassion.

Gay & Lesbian Couples Therapy

Gay and lesbian couples in committed relationships experience the same potential for joy, growth and pain as heterosexual couples do.

Eva’s training gives her a uniquely-valuable counseling perspective, based on 30 years of Gottman Relationship Institute Research following couples of all sexual orientations. Understanding the special challenges of gay and lesbian relationships, she is committed to teaching couples the new, proven techniques that can help solve problems and heal hurts, restoring intimacy and peace.

Here in a safe, inviting and welcoming environment, Eva offers new possibilities for growing together and creating a stronger, more loving couple bond.

Family Therapy

Each person has the ability to affect a family’s well-being for better or for worse.

When a teen acts out…when a parent falls ill…when a marriage flounders…reactions ripple throughout the family and are expressed differently by different family members. The results can be unruly or downright chaotic.

Recognizing the family’s interconnection, Eva uses Integrated Family System Therapy to help each family member understand the situation and his or her response to it, setting the stage for a healing “family reunion” in which the family returns to healthy functioning.

Cancer Support

Cancer patients, survivors, families, and caregivers: Eva has a special interest in helping people through the difficult times (and often, terrors) of those touched by cancer.

Finding the strength and resilience to deal with the “new reality” is not easy for anyone, as cancer affects far more than a person’s physical well-being. In fact, for many, it is also a major financial, relationship and spiritual stressor.

Eva’s goal is to empower those involved to “find a way”. A way to quell the fear. A way to maintain and strengthen supportive relationships. A way to find meaning in the journey. A way to make peace with “what is.” In short, a way to live as fully as possible right now, despite the diagnosis and the uncertainty of tomorrow.

Eva welcomes you with compassion and caring for this important and difficult time of life.
