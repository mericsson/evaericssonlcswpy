Education and Training

Eva Ericsson completed her initial training in Sweden, where she earned her Graduate Degree in Social work and Public Administration at the University of Gothenburg. Settling in the U.S., she devoted herself to her clinical practice, and to learning new ways to understand and be helpful.

To that end, she completed the Advanced Clinical Training Program in Marital and Family Therapy at The Family Institute (part of Northwestern University) and has recently completed the rigorous testing for Certification from the Gottman Relationship Institute in Seattle, WA. Created by much-lauded psychological researcher, John Gottman, this advanced approach is hailed as the first research-based method of couples therapy, offering both insight and proven blueprints for change.

Counseling Methods

Eva uses a combination of counseling methods, including traditional psychological (“talk”) therapy, cognitive therapy, and short-term solution-based therapy. Her goal is, always, to find and employ the most practical and effective ways to alleviate emotional pain, confusion, and depression.

Philosophy

“Therapy allows me to exercise my natural inclination to help people in pain, using both compassion and training. I am honored to share my experience, insights and support to help people better understand the choices they make…and the choices they might make to bring meaningful change to their lives.“
